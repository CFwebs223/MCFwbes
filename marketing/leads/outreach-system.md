## 1. Lead Targeting Criteria

### Target Industries (Priority Order)

| Priority | Industry | Why |
|----------|----------|-----|
| 1 | Restaurants & Cafes | High competition, menus change often, need online ordering |
| 2 | Local Service Businesses | Plumbers, electricians, locksmiths, cleaners, painters, landscapers, photographers |
| 3 | E-Commerce / Retail | Product-based businesses needing storefronts |
| 4 | Gyms & Fitness Studios | Class scheduling, membership signups, testimonials |
| 5 | Salons, Spas & Barbershops | Booking systems, portfolio displays, pricing pages |
| 6 | Professional Services | Lawyers, accountants, real estate agents, consultants |
| 7 | Trades & Construction | Builders, roofers, tilers, pool services |
| 8 | Health & Wellness | Dentists, physios, chiros, dieticians |
| 9 | Accommodation | B&Bs, guesthouses, lodges, self-catering |
| 10 | Non-Profits & Churches | Donation pages, event calendars, community info |

### Business Types to Target

- **Micro businesses** (1-5 employees) — most likely to have outdated or no website
- **Side hustles / passion projects** — founders invested but not yet professional online
- **Traditional businesses** — been around 5+ years, still using Facebook page as their "website"
- **Growing businesses** — getting traction but online presence holds them back

### Signals to Look For (Scoring System)

Red-hot signals (reach immediately):
- Facebook/Instagram page has "Website" link that goes nowhere or to a broken site
- Google Maps listing with no website URL
- Business listed on HelloPeter / Yelp with complaints about "can't find info online"
- Recently moved to a new location (need new site / landing page)
- Reopening under new ownership

Warm signals (reach this week):
- Website is clearly 5+ years old (dated design, "Copyright 2019" in footer)
- Website is not mobile-responsive
- No SSL certificate (shows "Not Secure" in browser)
- Facebook page only, no standalone website
- Competitors have better websites
- Business is running ads but landing page is poor

Cold signals (reach when pipeline is low):
- Any small business in target industry without a modern web presence
- Any business in a growth phase (adding locations, hiring)
- Seasonal businesses that should be capturing leads year-round

## 4. Phone Call Script

### Cold Call Flow

**Total target length: 2-3 minutes**

---

#### Step 1: Intro (10 seconds)

*Confident, warm, slightly slower than normal speech.*

**"Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] calling from MCF.Webs. I'm a web designer here in South Africa. You're not in trouble, I promise — I just have a quick observation about your business website, and I'd love 60 seconds to share it."**

*[Pause for response — they'll almost always say "sure" or "go ahead"]*

---

#### Step 2: The Hook (20 seconds)

**"I was looking at [Business Name] online, and here's what stood out: your actual service is great — your reviews prove that. But your website is the first impression a lot of customers get, and honestly? It's not showing you in the best light.**

**Specifically: [mention one specific issue — slow loading, not mobile-friendly, outdated design, no SSL].**

**In 2025, that matters more than ever because customers judge whether to trust you in about 3 seconds."**

---

#### Step 3: The Offer (15 seconds)

**"Here's why I'm calling — we build modern, mobile-friendly websites for SA businesses, and we keep the pricing honest. Most projects land between R2,000 and R8,000, completely built out, no monthly surprises."**

**"We can have a new site live in under two weeks."**

---

#### Step 4: The Call to Action (10 seconds)

**"I'd love to put together a simple one-page concept for [Business Name] — show you what it could look like. No cost, no obligation. If you like it, great. If not, no hard feelings.**

**Can I send that to your email? What's the best address?"**

## 8. Objection Handling Responses

### Objection 1: "We already have a website"

**Response:**
"I hear you. Most businesses do these days. But here's the thing — having a website and having one that actually works for you are two different things.

Can I ask — when was it built? Is it bringing in leads? Do customers ever tell you they couldn't find what they were looking for?

For R2k-R8k, we can build something that's not just a site — it's a lead-generating tool. And it'll be mobile-optimised, which most older sites aren't."

### Objection 2: "We don't have the budget right now"

**Response:**
"Completely understand — running a business is expensive. Let me ask though: how much is your current website costing you in missed business?

If you're losing even two or three customers a month because your site turns them away, you're losing more than our build cost. We're talking R2,000 to R8,000 once-off. That's less than most businesses spend on data or coffee in a month.

We can also structure payment — 50% upfront, 50% on completion — to make it easier."

### Objection 3: "I can build it myself with Wix/WordPress"

**Response:**
"You absolutely can — and lots of people do. But here's what I see most of the time: the DIY approach saves money upfront but costs in the long run. Slow load speeds, awkward layouts, no mobile optimisation, and a design that doesn't build trust.

We're R2k-R8k for a professionally designed, conversion-optimised site that's ready to go. That's less than the cost of a decent WordPress theme + plugins + your time to figure it out.

The question is: do you want a website, or do you want a website that makes you money?"

### Objection 4: "I need to think about it"

**Response:**
"No rush at all. Can I ask — what specifically do you need to think through? Is it the investment, the timing, or whether it's the right move?

If you'd like, I can send you a few examples of businesses in your industry that we've built for. Seeing it might make the decision easier. Either way, I'll follow up in a few days — no pressure."

### Objection 5: "Send me a quote"

**Response:**
"I'd love to. To make sure the quote is accurate for your business, can I ask a few quick questions?

1. How many pages would you need?

## 10. How to Find Business Emails & Phones for SA Businesses

### Method 1: Google Maps Scraping (Best for local businesses)

1. Go to Google Maps and search "[industry] in [city]" e.g., "restaurants in Cape Town"
2. Every listing typically includes:
   - Phone number (often displayed)
   - Website URL (if they have one)
   - Address
3. **Pro tip:** If no website is listed, that's a HOT lead — they're fully reliant on Google Maps
4. Use the business name + city to find their Facebook page, which often has a "Send Email" button or Messenger contact
5. Tools to help: Google Maps Extractor (Chrome extension), Phantombuster, or just copy-paste manually

### Method 2: Facebook / Instagram Search

1. Search the business on Facebook or Instagram
2. Check their "About" section — many SA businesses list their email and phone
3. Check their Facebook Page transparency section for the business email
4. If no email is listed, check if they have a "Book Now" or "Contact Us" button — these often link to WhatsApp
5. If they respond to DMs, just message them on the platform first

### Method 3: Company Website (the obvious one)

If they have a website:
- Look for a "Contact Us" page
- Common SA email formats: info@[business].co.za, [name]@[business].co.za, admin@[business].co.za
- Check the website footer — many small SA businesses list email and phone there
- Whois lookup on the domain (whois.co.za) sometimes reveals admin contact info

### Method 4: SA-Specific Directories

- **SA Yellow Pages** (yellowpages.co.za) — lists phone numbers for most registered SA businesses
- **HelloPeter** (hellopeter.com) — business pages often list contact info
- **BizCommunity** (bizcommunity.com) — good for professional services
- **NetFlorist / SweepSouth / UberEats** — businesses listed on these platforms often have public contact info
- **Snupit** — SA trades and services directory with contact details
- **Cape Business News / SA Business Directory** — local business listings

### Method 5: Email Discovery Tools

These are ethical tools for finding business emails:

- **Hunter.io** — enter domain, get email addresses associated with that domain. Free tier: 25 searches/month
- **Snov.io** — similar to Hunter, good for SA domains
- **Apollo.io** — CRM with built-in email finder, good for SA leads
- **Anymail Finder** — works well with South African domains
- **Pro tip:** Most SA small businesses use Gmail. Try [businessname]@gmail.com or info.[businessname]@gmail.com

### Method 6: Manual Email Guessing (Generic Formats for SA Businesses)

Try these patterns based on what you know:

### Method 7: Phone Number Discovery

- **WhatsApp Business directories** — search for the business on WhatsApp
- **Facebook Business Pages** — the "Call Now" button reveals the number

### Method 8: LinkedIn for Professional Services

1. Search for the business on LinkedIn
2. Find the owner or manager's profile
3. Many SA professionals list their phone number or email in their LinkedIn "Contact Info" section
4. If their company page has a "Website" button, click through and grab contact info
5. **Sales Navigator tip:** Use filters for "Company Headcount: 1-10" + "Industry" + "Location: South Africa"

### Email Verification Checklist

Before sending any email, verify it:

1. Use **Hunter.io** email verification (free)

4. **DO NOT** send to obviously fake or guessed emails — getting marked as spam hurts your domain reputation
