# 1. Qualification Questions

## Purpose
Determine if the lead is a fit before investing discovery call time. Never pitch before qualifying. Qualification happens on the initial call or DM exchange.

## The Four Qualification Pillars (B.T.N.)

### B — Budget

Ask naturally, after building rapport:

> *"Just so I can point you to the right option — and I won't try to upsell you, I promise — what budget did you have in mind for getting this done?"*

If they avoid:

> *"Totally understand. Our packages range from R2,000 for a digital menu all the way up to R8,000 for a premium 3D scrolling site. Does that feel like the right ballpark for what you're looking for?"*

### T — Timeline

> *"And when were you hoping to have this live? Are we talking next week, next month, or are you still just exploring?"*

Clarify urgency levels:

| Response | Classification |
|---|---|
| "ASAP" / "Yesterday" | Hot — close this week |
| "Within a month" | Warm — nurture with deadlines |
| "Just looking" / "No rush" | Cold — add to nurture sequence, don't chase |

### N — Needs (Scope matching)

> *"Tell me a bit about your business. What's the main thing you want this website to do for you? Is it about getting new customers, showing off your work, replacing a menu, or something else?"*

Then drill down:

> *"How many pages would you need?"*
> *"Do you need a booking or ordering system?"*
> *"Will you want to update it yourself, or do you want us to handle updates?"*
> * "Who is your ideal customer — walk-ins, appointment-based, e-commerce?"*

### Decision Authority

Always ask this before the call ends:

> *"And just so I know who else needs to be involved — are you the decision-maker on this, or will you need to run it past anyone else?"*

## Package Fit Matrix

| Answer Pattern | Recommended Package |
|---|---|
| Restaurant / takeaway / coffee shop | Digital Menu (R2k) |
| Small service business (plumber, salon, DJ) | Classic Site (R5k) |
| Portfolio / premium brand / property / agency | 3D Scroll Site (R8k) |
| "I want the best" / "Make it impressive" | 3D Scroll Site (R8k) |
| "Just something simple and cheap" | Digital Menu (R2k) |
| "Something in between" | Classic Site (R5k) |

# 2. Discovery Call Structure

## The 5-Stage Framework

Total call length: 20-30 minutes.
Split: 60% listening, 40% guiding.

---

### Stage 1: Rapport & Frame (3 minutes)

Open with warmth but authority. You are the expert. They came to you.

> *"Hey [Name], thanks for jumping on the call. I know you're busy so I'll be respectful of your time. I've looked at [business name] a bit — looks like you guys do [X]. I'm keen to hear what's happening and whether a website could move the needle for you. But first — how's business been this last quarter?"*

Listen to their answer. Acknowledge honestly. Do not fake enthusiasm about bad months.

> *"I appreciate you sharing that. That's exactly why I think we can help."*

### Stage 2: Pain Discovery (7 minutes)

This is the most important stage. Ask, then shut up. Let silence do the work.

> *"Walk me through your current situation with your online presence. What's working, what's not, what's embarrassing you?"*

Follow-up probes (use 2-3, based on their answers):

> *"When a potential customer looks you up right now — what do they find?"*

> *"Have you ever lost a customer because they couldn't find what they needed online?"*

> *"How much time are you spending answering the same questions over the phone that a website could answer for you?"*

> *"If your website could do one thing perfectly — what would that one thing be?"*

> *"Be honest with me — on a scale of 1 to 10, how proud are you of your current online presence?"*

When they say 6 or below:

> *"That's what I was afraid of. Let me show you how we fix that."*

### Stage 3: Vision Building (5 minutes)

Paint the picture of what life looks like after the site goes live. Do NOT talk features yet.

> *"Imagine this: It's a month from now. A customer in [their area] searches for [their service]. Your site loads immediately — it's fast, it's beautiful, it's professional. They see exactly what you offer, they see your work, they see your prices. They pick up the phone and call you. That call alone pays for the site. How would that feel?"*

Tailor to their package:

**Digital Menu (R2k):**
> *"Your customers walk in, scan the QR code on the table, and the menu loads instantly on their phone. Full-screen, beautiful photos of your food, no PDF, no zooming, no hassle. They order faster, they enjoy the experience more, and they come back. That's R2,000 well spent, isn't it?"*

**Classic Site (R5k):**
> *"This is your 24/7 salesperson. The site works while you sleep. It answers questions, it collects leads, it builds trust. Your competitors might have a Facebook page. You'll have a professional web presence that makes you look ten times bigger than you are."*

**3D Scroll (R8k):**
> *"This is the kind of site people stop and stare at. It's immersive. It tells a story. When a potential client lands on your site, they don't leave — they scroll, they explore, they're impressed before they've even spoken to you. That's the power of a premium 3D experience. It positions you as the market leader, not just another player."*

### Stage 4: Package Presentation (5 minutes)

Present their recommended package with conviction. Do not sound apologetic about price.

> *"Based on everything you've told me, here's what I'd recommend for [business name]: The [Package Name] at R[Price]."*

State the price once. Do not explain it. Do not justify it. Then pause.

Say nothing. Let them respond first.

If they stay silent (they will), follow with:

> *"And here's exactly what that gets you:"*
> * Bullet points: deliverables, timeline, what's included.
> * Immediately follow with a benefit statement.

**Package Specifics:**

**Digital Menu — R2,000:**
> *"This includes a fully designed digital menu with your branding, all your menu items with descriptions and pricing, high-quality food photography integration, QR code generation for tables, mobile-first design, hosting for the first year, and two rounds of revisions. We can have this live in 3-5 business days."*

**Classic Site — R5,000:**
> *"Up to a 5-page responsive website with custom design, mobile optimization, contact forms, social media integration, Google Maps, basic SEO setup, hosting for the first year, and professional email setup assistance. We go live in 7-10 business days."*

**3D Scroll Site — R8,000:**
> *"A premium one-page scrolling experience with custom 3D visuals and parallax effects, bespoke animations, full mobile responsiveness, lead capture forms, SEO optimization, hosting for the first year, priority support, and a hand-crafted design that reflects your brand at the highest level. Timeline is 10-14 business days."*

### Stage 5: Next Steps & Commit (2-5 minutes)

> *"So here's what I suggest. I'll put together a formal proposal and a mockup concept based on what we discussed. I'll send that over to you by [day/time]. We can review it together, and if you're happy, we can get started right away. Sound good?"*

Commit to a specific follow-up time:

> *"I'm going to send the proposal tomorrow morning. Let's set a call for [day] at [time] to walk through it. If you love it, we start. If something's off, we adjust. Either way, you walk away with clarity. Fair?""

# 3. Proposal Presentation Script

## The Setup

The proposal is sent BEFORE the call. The call is to close.

On the call:

> *"Did you have a chance to look through the proposal I sent?"*

Wait for answer.

**If Yes:**

> *"Great. What stood out to you most?"*

Let them tell you what they liked. Then close from there.

**If No:**

> *"No problem at all. I know you're busy. Let me walk you through the highlights — it'll only take 5 minutes. Sound good?"*

## The Presentation Flow

> *"Here's what I've put together for [business name]. This isn't a template — this is built specifically around what you told me you needed."*

Walk through each section:
1. **Problem** — restate their pain (shows you listened)
2. **Solution** — the package you selected
3. **Deliverables** — exactly what they get
4. **Timeline** — when they get it
5. **Investment** — the price (single number, clear)
6. **Guarantee** — revisions included, satisfaction-focused

## The Price Reveal

> *"Your total investment for this is R[Price]. That includes everything I've shown you — design, development, hosting for the first year, and two rounds of revisions. There are no hidden fees, no monthly surprises."*

Then: **SHUT UP.**

The first person to speak after the price loses. Count to ten in your head if you need to. Let the silence hang.

## After They Respond

**Positive response (hesitation but interest):**

> *"This is a no-pressure situation. You're not buying a template — you're investing in a professional web presence that will bring you customers. If we agree it's the right move, I can have you live in [timeline]. What's holding you back?""

**Enthusiastic response:**

> *"Amazing. Let's get you started. I'll send over the payment link and our brief intake form. Once I receive those, I'll kick off the design process. You're going to love this."*

# 4. Price Objection Handling

## The Mindset

Do not discount. Do not apologize. The price is the price. You compete on quality, speed, and service — not on being the cheapest.

## The Framework: A.C.E.

**Acknowledge** the objection — validate their concern.
**Confirm** the value — restate what they get.
**Escape** to a decision — reframe the choice.

---

### Objection 1: "It's too expensive" / "That's out of my budget"

> *"I hear you. And I appreciate you being upfront about that. Let me ask you this — what were you expecting to pay?"*

They will usually name a lower number or say "I don't know."

> *"Look, I can build you something cheaper. But here's what happens with cheap websites: they're slow, they don't work properly on phones, they don't get updated, and in six months you're embarrassed to send people to them. That's not a good deal at any price. What I'm offering you is a site that actually works — that actually brings you customers. And at R[Price], it pays for itself with one or two new clients. Can I show you how?"*

**SA Variant:**

> *"Ek verstaan. Look, I know R[Price] feels like a lot right now. But let me put it in perspective — how much do you spend on [relevant business expense — stock, advertising, rent, fuel] in a month? For less than that, you get a website that works 24/7, never calls in sick, and never asks for a raise. That's a pretty good deal."*

---

### Objection 2: "I need to think about it"

> *"I respect that. Let me ask — what specifically do you need to think through? Is it the price, the timing, or are you not sure this is the right fit?"*

Listen to their answer. Then:

> *"Here's what I'd suggest. Let's not overthink this. The longer you wait, the more potential customers land on whatever you have now — or worse, they look for you and find nothing. Let's get you started. I'll send the payment link and we can have the first draft ready by [day]. If you don't love it, we'll make it right. Does that work?"*

---

### Objection 3: "I can do it myself with Wix/WordPress"

> *"You absolutely can. And some people do. But here's the reality — by the time you buy a template, pay for hosting, spend 20 hours figuring it out, and end up with something that looks 'okay' — you've spent more than R[Price] in your own time. More importantly, a DIY site looks like a DIY site. Your customers can tell. Is that really the impression you want to make?"*

---

### Objection 4: "I'll wait until next month / after the holidays"

> *"I understand the timing concern. Here's the thing — we only take on [X] projects a month to maintain quality. If you wait, there's no guarantee I'll have a slot when you're ready. Lock in now at today's rate, and we can schedule the start date for when it works for you. That way you don't lose momentum, and you don't lose the slot."*

---

### Objection 5: "Can you do it for R[X] less?"

> *"I appreciate you asking. Look, I want this to work for you. But my price is my price. What I won't do is give you a discount and then cut corners — because you'd be the one who suffers. What I can do is offer you a payment plan: [propose split payment — e.g., 50% upfront, 50% on completion]. That way it's easier on cash flow without sacrificing quality. How does that sound?"*

---

## Price Objection Cheat Sheet

| Objection | Response Strategy |
|---|---|
| "Too expensive" | Reframe as investment, compare to monthly business cost |
| "Thinking about it" | Uncover real objection, create urgency |
| "Can I DIY?" | Time/value gap — your time is worth more |
| "Next month" | Slot scarcity + price lock-in |
| "Can you discount?" | Offer payment plan, never discount |
| "My cousin does websites" | Professionalism gap — see competitor section |
| "I'll come back" | Set a specific follow-up, don't let them disappear |

# 5. Competitor Objection Handling

## The Principles

1. Never trash competitors. It makes you look insecure.
2. Acknowledge their validity, then differentiate.
3. Lead with YOUR strengths, not competitor weaknesses.

---

### Wix / Squarespace / DIY Builders

When they mention using a builder:

> *"Wix is great for getting something up quickly. And for some businesses, it works. But here's the difference: with Wix, you're renting a template. With us, you get a custom-designed site built specifically for your brand — not a template that a thousand other businesses are also using. Plus, when something breaks or needs updating, you're on your own with Wix. With us, you've got a direct line to the person who built it. That peace of mind is worth the investment."*

---

### Freelancers on Upwork / Facebook / Student Designers

> *"There are a lot of people offering cheap websites. And listen — I started as a freelancer too. The difference is: a freelancer builds you a site and moves on. You're their side project. I run a dedicated web design agency. I have a process, a portfolio, a track record, and I stand behind my work. When you hire me, you're not just buying a website — you're buying accountability. If something goes wrong, I'm here. Can a freelancer on R500 promise you that?"*

---

### Other Web Agencies (Large)

> *"There are bigger agencies out there. And if you need a R50,000 site with a full marketing team, go with them. But for what you need — a beautiful, functional, professional website — those agencies will charge you 3-5 times what I charge for the same quality. You're paying for their overhead, their office rent, their account managers. With us, you pay for the work. You get senior-level design at a fraction of the cost."*

---

### "My Current Site Is Fine"

> *"I'm glad it works for you. Let me ask — when was the last time you updated it? And when you look at your competitors' sites, how does yours compare? Because your customers are comparing. They're judging your professionalism based on what they see online. If your site looks dated, they assume your business is dated too. Let me show you what modern looks like — no obligation."*

---

### Social Media Only ("I just use Facebook")

> *"Facebook is good for engagement. But it's not your property. If Meta changes the algorithm, changes the layout, or shuts down tomorrow — your entire online presence is gone. A website is your digital property. It's the one place online that you fully own and control. Think of Facebook as the billboard, and your website as the actual store. You need both — but the store is where the business happens."*

# 6. Urgency Creation Scripts

## The Psychology

Urgency must be genuine or at least feel genuine. Never lie about availability. If you say you have limited slots, actually limit your slots.

---

### Limited Slots Script

> *"I'll be honest with you — I only take on [3-5] new clients a month. That's it. I don't do volume, I do quality. Right now I have [1-2] slots open for this month. After that, the next available start date would be [next month]. If you want to get your site live before [deadline relevant to them], we need to move on this this week."*

---

### Seasonal / Offer-Based Urgency

> *"I'm running a special for [month/quarter] — the [Package Name] includes [bonus: extra page, free SEO audit, free revision round] at no extra cost. That offer expires on [date]. After that, the price goes back to standard. I don't want you to miss out because you didn't know."*

**Seasonal Offer Ideas:**

| Season | Offer |
|---|---|
| January | "New Year, New Site" — free business email setup |
| June/July | "Mid-Year Refresh" — free SEO audit |
| September | "Spring Special" — free social media kit |
| November | "Festive Ready" — free holiday banner design |
| EOFY | "Tax Season" — R500 off any package (valid with invoice for tax purposes) |

---

### Loss Aversion Script

> *"Here's what I don't want to happen: I don't want you to wait, then forget, then three months from now you're still stuck with a site you're not proud of — and in that time, you've lost potential customers who looked for you and found nothing useful. Let's just get this started. The hardest part is deciding to do it — and you've already done that part."*

---

### Pre-Price-Increase Urgency

> *"I should mention — our prices are going up on [date]. I'm grandfathering all current clients at today's rates. If you lock in now, you'll always pay today's price, even for future projects. That's worth considering."*

---

### Momentum Closing Script

> *"You've done the hard work. You've done the research. You've jumped on this call. You're clearly serious about growing your business. Don't let that momentum die. Let's keep the ball rolling. I can have a draft ready for you by [day]. All I need is the green light. Shall I send you the payment link?""

# 8. Closing Techniques

## 10 Techniques Adapted for Web Services

---

### Technique 1: The Assumptive Close

Act as if they've already decided. Speak in future tense. Do not ask "if" — ask "which" or "when."

> *"Great. So I'll get started on the design this week. I'll send you the first draft by Thursday. Do you prefer email or WhatsApp for draft reviews?"*

> *"To get your site live by [date], I'll need the go-ahead by tomorrow. Should I send the payment link to your email or WhatsApp?"*

**When to use:** After handling objections, when they're warm but hesitant.

---

### Technique 2: The Ben Franklin Close

List pros and cons explicitly. Guide them to see the weight of inaction.

> *"Let's do this quickly. Grab a pen or just think with me. On one side — the reasons NOT to move forward. Maybe it's the cost, maybe it's the timing. On the other side — what you get: a professional website that works 24/7, brings in customers, makes you look credible, and costs less than what you probably spend on coffee in a month. Now be honest — which side weighs more?"*

**When to use:** Logical, analytical prospects who need to justify the decision to themselves.

---

### Technique 3: The Sharp Angle Close

When they ask for a concession, immediately make it conditional on closing now.

**They say:** "Can you include an extra page?"
**You say:** *"I can add that in — if we lock this in today. Otherwise I'd have to charge extra for scope changes."*

**They say:** "Can you do it in a week?"
**You say:** *"I can prioritize your project — if you confirm today. Normally it's [X] days, but I can move things around for an immediate start."*

**When to use:** When they ask for extras or faster timelines.

---

### Technique 4: The Puppy Dog Close

Let them experience the result before committing fully.

> *"Here's what I'll do. I'll build your homepage concept — no charge, no obligation. If you love it, we continue with the full site. If you don't, you walk away with a free homepage concept and we're still friends. Fair?"*

**When to use:** High-ticket hesitation on the 3D Scroll or Classic Site.

**SA Context:** This works well because South Africans appreciate low-risk offers. Use sparingly — only when you're confident the design will impress.

---

### Technique 5: The Now-or-Never Close

Create genuine limited availability.

> *"Here's the thing — I've got two slots left this month. One is yours if you want it. After that, the next available date is [date]. I can't guarantee the same turnaround time if we wait. If you want to move forward, tell me now and I'll block that slot for you."*

**When to use:** When they've been stalling or you sense they're shopping around.

---

### Technique 6: The Question Close

Ask leading questions that require a "yes" to continue — building momentum toward yes.

> *"Do you think your business would benefit from more customers finding you online?" [Yes]*

> *"Do you agree that a professional website builds more trust than a Facebook page?" [Yes]*

> *"And do you think R[Price] is a fair investment for a site that brings you new customers 24/7?" [Yes]*

> *"Great. Then let's get started. I'll send the payment link now."*

**When to use:** With indecisive prospects who need to talk themselves into it.

---

### Technique 7: The Takeaway Close

Pull back the offer to trigger loss aversion. People want what they can't have.

> *"You know what — I don't think you're ready for this yet. And that's okay. I'd rather you come back when you're fully committed. I'm going to close your file for now. When the time is right, give me a call and I'll see if I have availability. Fair?"*

Most of the time, they will object and say "No wait, I am interested."

**When to use:** With pushback-heavy prospects or time-wasters.

---

### Technique 8: The Summary Close

Recap all the value they've agreed to during the conversation. Cumulative yeses.

> *"Let me just recap what we've discussed:*
> *- You need a website that looks professional and brings in customers.*
> *- A DIY site won't give you the quality you want.*
> *- You need it live within [timeline].*
> *- The [Package Name] covers everything you need.*
> *- The price is R[Price] with hosting included.*
> *- And I've shown you how it pays for itself with one new client.*
>
> *Is there anything I've missed that would stop us from moving forward?"*

**When to use:** End of a long conversation. Reaffirms all the value points.

---

### Technique 9: The Visualisation Close

Get them to imagine themselves after the site is live.

> *"Close your eyes for a second. It's [one month from now]. Your site is live. A customer in [their area] finds you on Google. They scroll through your site, they see your work, they're impressed. They pick up the phone and call you. That first call — that first new customer — comes from this website right here. How does that feel?"*

Pause.

> *"That feeling is worth more than R[Price]. Isn't it?"*

**When to use:** Emotional buyers, creative professionals, restaurant owners who care about experience.

---

### Technique 10: The Payment Plan Close

Remove the price barrier by splitting the payment.

> *"If the upfront payment is the only thing stopping you, here's what I can do: 50% now to get started, 50% when we go live. That way you're not out of pocket until you see the finished product. Does that work for you?"*

**When to use:** Price objections where they genuinely want it but cash flow is tight.

**SA Context:** Many small business owners run month-to-month. Payment plans are standard in SA. Offer them freely but frame it as a favour, not a policy.

---

## Closing Technique Decision Tree

| Prospect Type | Technique to Lead With |
|---|---|
| Logical, analytical | Ben Franklin Close or Summary Close |
| Emotional, visual | Visualisation Close or Puppy Dog Close |
| Indecisive, quiet | Question Close or Assumptive Close |
| Pushy, argumentative | Takeaway Close or Sharp Angle Close |
| Price-sensitive | Payment Plan Close or Now-or-Never Close |
| "I need to think" | Summary Close then Question Close |

# 9. Onboarding Call Script

## Timing: Immediately after payment is confirmed.

### Purpose
Set expectations, collect information, build confidence in the decision, and reduce buyer's remorse.

---

## Script

### Step 1: Congratulations & Reassurance (2 minutes)

> *"Hey [Name]! Congratulations — you've made a great decision for [business name]. I'm excited to get started on this. # 10. Referral Request System

## The Mindset

Your best clients come from existing clients. Referrals should be systematic, not random.

## Timing

Ask for referrals at the HIGHEST point of satisfaction. Do not ask immediately after close — wait until they've seen value.

**Best times to ask:**
1. When they say "Wow, this looks amazing" (during draft review)
2. At the 30-day post-launch check-in
3. When they tell you they got a lead from the site
4. When they refer to you in a positive context

---

## The Scripts

### At Launch (Highest Excitement)

> *"I'm so glad you love the site. Listen — I'm always looking for more business owners like you who value quality. If you know any other restaurant owners / business owners who could use a proper website, I'd really appreciate an introduction. No pressure — just if it feels right."*

### 30-Day Post-Launch Check-In (Value Proven)

> **Subject:** One month live — how's it going?
>
> Hey [Name],
>
> It's been a month since your site went live. I wanted to check in — how's it going? Have you seen any new traffic or leads coming through?
>
> Also — one quick favour. If you know anyone who's been asking about getting a website, I'd love an introduction. I help people who care about quality. If they're serious, I'll take good care of them.
>
> Thanks for trusting me with your site. Let me know if you need anything.
>
> Best,
> [Your Name]
> MCF Webs

### The "Just Got a Lead" Referral Request

When they tell you they got business from the site:

> *"That's incredible to hear. That one lead paid for the site, didn't it? [Pause] Hey, quick question — you clearly have friends and colleagues in the same space. If you think any of them would benefit from the same kind of result, I'd be grateful for the introduction. I promise I'll treat them the same way I treated you."*
